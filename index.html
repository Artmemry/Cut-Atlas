    How to read the maps. Each map shows how that country actually divides the carcass — the boundaries differ, not just the labels. Switch the language selector and watch the lines move. A cut in the list is anchored to where its muscle sits, so it stays put while the map around it redraws; the region tag tells you which primal it falls under in the current scheme.

    On accuracy. Cuts don't translate one-to-one, and these maps are stylised schematics, not anatomical drawings — close enough to find your way at the counter, not a butchery manual. Where a name is the closest regional equivalent rather than an identical cut, it's marked “≈”.
